HUMAN ASHES TO DIAMONDS?!

What is a diamond? It’s just a rock, like any other found in a playground. The only difference is that it’s pretty and shiny.

TThe diamond is a type of igneous rock known as kimberlite. It is the only gem made of a single element: carbon. Typically, a diamond is 99.95% carbon and the other 0.05% includes one or more trace elements. The entire process of diamond formation takes between 1 billion and 3.3 billion years, which is approximately 25% to 75% of the earth’s age.

There are speculations that natural diamond production will not be possible after 2050 as profitable deposits would have run out. One very interesting solution is to produce diamonds from human ashes.

Growing diamonds in the lab essentially involves using carbon, heat and pressure above the ground as opposed to way below the ground as in the formation of natural diamonds. Pure carbon is extracted from human ashes with the help of a graphite crucible. This pure carbon is turned into diamond in advanced machines that simulate growing conditions like under the earth – high temperature and high pressure. The output of this machine is a raw diamond – in its infant stage – uncut and unpolished. This production can take between 3 to 9 months which is definitely better than a billion years. The raw diamond is then cut, polished and colored by professionals and voila – we just turned human ashes into a magnificent diamond.
