CARBON OFFSETS

Many major companies have made some surprising claims about their climate impact such as net- zero by 2035 or sooner but when you look at how they are doing that its not always by decreasing their own carbon emissions. They do it by using what is called a carbon offset.

This concept relies on the fact that global warming is global. A green house gas regardless of where it is emitted from can be anywhere in the world with in 7-8 days. That means a company with a carbon footprint can offset that by planting a certain number of trees each year or building a solar power plant or even just investing in climate research since that research will eventually help us reduce emissions later. there are a huge amount of things that are considered carbon offsets and these claims made by companies is largely unverified.

Disney is a company that accomplishes its claims largely by carbon offsets but make no mistake it is not carbon neutral because trees eventually burn, power plants retire and research fails. Even if the dreamworld picture happens and the offsets work all the time in the end of the day they can only achieve a 50% reduction in emissions.

Carbon offsets can help (if done right) but it cannot get us to net zero. For that we need to decrease our emissions and capture the rest from the atmosphere through carbon capture systems.
