Diogenes- The World’s First Hippie

‘He has the most who is most content with the least.’

Diogenes, often referred to as ‘Diogenes the Cynic’, was a Greek philosopher and one of the founders of Cynic philosophy. Diogenes lived in a barrel without any worldly possessions and rejected societal norms. Diogenes felt that by rejecting the opinions of society and it’s measures of success he could truly be free. Diogenes would roam around Athens with a lantern in hopes of finding an ‘honest man’. Diogenes didn’t consider himself as belonging to a country but instead believed he was a ‘citizen of the world.’

While most people today would look at Diogenes as a lunatic, much of his work laid the foundation for cynic philosophy. Diogenes is also remembered for his quips and witty comebacks. Once when Diogenes was laying in the Sun, Alexander approached him and asked Diogenes if there was something he could do, to which Diogenes replied, ‘Stand out of my light.’ Diogenes belongs to the rare breed of men who practice what they preach. He truly believed in what he professed and is famous for saying, ‘It is the privilege of the Gods to want nothing, and of godlike men to want little.’

Whether or not you agree with Diogenes’ style of living there are certainly some aspects of his life that we can learn from. He questioned the status quo and that’s an example we can still follow. Not to blindly follow conventional or majority views but to think hard about what is truly valuable. Thinking critically of our institutions and way of life is more important than ever.
