A Five Chambered Heart

Five-chambered heart or Cor-triatriatum is an extremely rare but potentially lethal cardiac anomaly developed, there is a small extra chamber above the left or right atrium of the heart, during the embryonic period (week 5-week 11 of gestation). It is estimated to occur in 0.5 per 100000 live births. True congenital aneurysm is usually associated with other birth malformation such as ventricular diverticula(a outpouching structure from heart wall), septal defect (hole in the wall that divides the atria), malformation of thoraco abdominal aorta and other malformations.

It was first reported in 1868. Cor-triatriatum, a heart with 3 atria (triatrial heart), is a congenital development in which the left atrium (cor triatriatum sinistrum) or right atrium (cor triatriatum dextrum) is divided into 2 parts by a fold of tissue, a membrane, or a fibromuscular band. However, variable types of subtotal cor triatriatum are also noted, with only the right or left pulmonary veins draining into the upper chamber.

The person may be asymptomatic for many years after birth, or so severe that it is life threatening. Heart defects are the leading cause of birth defect-related deaths. The optimal treatment still remains unclear ; depending on clinical presentation , mode of detection and accompanying abnormalities which will determine the prognosis. However; the use of echocardiography has improved the description of congenital heart diseases and their early diagnosis. This has led to dramatic increases in survival of children with serious heart defects.
