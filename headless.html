HEADLESS BABY

In medical terms known as anencephaly which is a birth defect where the baby is born without the parts of the brain and skull. It occurs when the upper part of the neural tube does not close all the way, necessary for the formation of the brain and skull, spinal cord and backbones. Approximately 100 thousand cases of anencephaly occur per annum.

So, what exactly happens in such an unusual case? Most of the time the baby dies before birth in the motherâ€™s womb due to lack of vital organ systems. In cases where the baby manages to survive through the gestation period, it happens to come out stillborn. So, ideally this defect is considered fatal.

However, there are situations where miraculously the baby manages to survive and is born healthy with respect to all other aspects. But, faces several other complications such as the loss of hearing ability, unconsciousness, blindness, inability to feel pain etc. The longest living child with this congenital defect survived for 28 months without any major requirement for life sustaining interventions. This being a truly exceptional case as most live for a maximum of 2-3 hours.

There is no precise cure or treatment for anencephaly but according to research consumption of folic acid before and during pregnancy may help prevent this unfortunate defect. Therefore, early detection might be the best way to tackle anencephaly.
