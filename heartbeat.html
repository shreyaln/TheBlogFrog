THE HEARTBEAT MACHINE

The first clinically successful Cardiac Pacemaker was invented in 1960; which meant that there was now one less cause of death. It is a medical device that generates electrical impulses delivered by electrodes to the heart so as to regulate the heartbeat.

What naturally causes the heartbeat? Situated in the myocardium is the Electrical conduction system of the heart which transmits electrical signals generated by the sinoatrial node (group of cells in the wall of the right atrium) to cause contraction of the heart muscle.

The pacemaker comes into the picture either because the heart’s natural pacemaker is not fast enough, or because there is a block in the heart’s electrical conduction system. There exists a separate field of science called Cardiac electrophysiology dealing with the electrical activities of the heart. A pacemaker comprises a pulse generator which contains the battery and electric circuitry, and leads which are one to three insulated wires placed in the heart chamber(s).

The Pacemaker is generally implanted in the upper-front chest close to the left or right shoulder. The tip of the lead or leads (wires) is inserted either into the right ventricle, right atrium or coronary sinus through a large vein, guided by X-ray imaging, depending on the type of pacemaker. When the batteries of the pacemaker run out (5-10 years after implantation), the generator is replaced while the leads stay the same.

When the pacemaker lead does not detect electrical activity in the ventricle or atrium - within a normal beat-to-beat period - it will stimulate either the ventricle or the atrium with a short low voltage pulse. If it does sense electrical activity, it will hold off on stimulating. This is the function of a basic pacemaker. The more complex forms include the ability to sense and/or stimulate both the atrial and ventricular chambers. In the future, machine learning algorithms can be in pacemakers so as to distribute their effect across multiple places in the heart in a synchronised and precise fashion.
