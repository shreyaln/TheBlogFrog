WHAT IS THE INTERNET

A large percentage of humans don’t actually know how the internet works. Our perspective of the internet is not very physical, it is virtual. But, the internet is comprised of mostly hardware – wires.

All text, images, videos, etc. are physically stored in huge data centers around the world. This data reaches our devices through what we call the internet. You may think we receive this data with the help of satellites, but this isn’t the case. Satellites, thousands of miles above the earth’s surface, don’t provide the most efficient way for data transmission. The actual way in which the data reaches our devices is through a complex network of optical fiber cables. Data travels in the form of light pulses, through the optical fiber cables, often over hills and under the sea to reach us. A few global companies lay and maintain these optical fiber networks, such as Verizon and Google. These cables are stretched across the seabed to reach your home router or a cell tower. The router or cell tower converts these light signals to electromagnetic signals which your device receives.

How are these cables more efficient than the satellites considering the huge distance the signals have to travel either way? Data is sent to your devices in the form of a large number of zeros and ones. But these zeros and ones are not transmitted in one big chunk; instead they are cut into small packets of information (zeros and ones). Each packet individually takes the best route available to it in order to reach your device faster. It is not necessary that all the packets take the same route and hence the cables are efficient.
