SLEEPING SOLVES PROBLEMS

Turns out that the phrase, ‘Sleep on it’ has scientific credibility.

The brain can never be turned off; it is always active. During the day, we consciously use our brain to think about various things. While we are asleep, the brain goes through the events of that day and sees what is left unfinished.

The prefrontal cortex ( handles rational thinking and impulse control) shuts down. Neuromodulators norepinephrine and serotonin are turned off. Norepinephrine is a stress hormone that enhances our focus on immediate, concrete problems. Serotonin is a hormone that modulates mood and cognition. Due to this, there is no longer a critical edge or categories to put ideas in. The brain can now identify those connections and loose information that a conscious mind would dismiss.

Suppose you wake up one day and you have a ‘gut feeling’ that you just can’t seem to explain. This is a result of processing background information accumulated throughout the day while you sleep. There is no point in trying to do this during the day or when you are extremely stressed because the norepinephrine and serotonin prevents you from doing so.

Have you ever noticed that your dreams don’t ever last hours and hours long. Even in that small portion of the dream you can recall, you can tell that it didn’t go on for hours. This is because dreams are thinking or problem-solving in a different biochemical state of waking.In order to harness sleep’s problem-solving powers to the fullest, it may help to think about your dilemma just before bed. This may encourage your sleeping brain to go to work on it. No matter how much you try to relax when you are awake to think of solutions to some problem, it wont compare to the productivity you would achieve during the REM stage of sleep.
