WHY DON'T PLANTS GROW ON THE BEACH

Everyone just intuitively seems to know that plants can grow only in certain types of soil. Oftentimes, we are unable to explain such intuition. A few days back, my 6-year-old cousin asked me why plants don’t grow on the beach. How would I explain it to a 6-year-old?

Instead of trying to explain the difference in presence of nutrients in each type of soil, I decided to show him via experiments. We collected soil from a local park, my garden, as well as from the outskirts of my city (red soil).

In a laboratory, we conducted certain experiments to test the presence of various nutrients in them and he witnessed exactly how each of those soils were different from each other.

Most soil may look the same, but we proved that everything is definitely not what it seems.

Inspired by this experiment, I began researching more about the best soil type to grow certain plants. This led to my latest venture – collecting soil samples from farms around my city and telling the farmers what kind and how much fertilizer to use to maximize plant growth.
