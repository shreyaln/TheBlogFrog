TWO HUMANS IN ONE

Is it possible for a man to have 2 brains?

Our brain has 2 hemispheres connected by the Corpus Callosum, through which they communicate and coordinate actions and decisions. This is necessary because each hemisphere has different functions. In a cognitive sense, the right hemisphere is responsible for recognizing objects and timing, and in an emotional sense it is responsible for empathy, humor and depression. The left hemisphere controls the primary sensory functions of the right side of the body and is responsible for scientific and math skills, and logic.

The concept of splitting a brain into two is carried out through a procedure called Corpus Callosotomy. This results in partial or complete disconnection between the 2 hemispheres. This procedure is usually used as a last resort measure to treat intractable Epilepsy. While the patient may stop experiencing seizures after the procedure, their brain will not be one anymore. Each hemisphere will have its own perception, concepts and impulses to act on. Communication between the two sides of the brain is inhibited. When split-brain patients are shown an image only in the left half of each eye's visual field, they cannot vocally name what they have seen. This is because the image seen in the left visual field is sent only to the right side of the brain and most people’s speech control center is in the left hemisphere.

Although drastic conflicts are rare, they are not impossible. For example, when one split-brain patient dressed himself, he sometimes pulled his pants up with one hand and down with the other – one side of his brain wanted him to get dressed and the other side did not. The same patient reported to have grabbed his wife violently in his left hand while his right hand came to her aid and grabbed the aggressive left hand. Usually when such a conflict arises, one hemisphere overrides the other. A spilt brain patient must not be mistaken with a split-personality patient. The split-personality patient deals with a psychological problem whereas the split-brain patient deals with a physical one.
